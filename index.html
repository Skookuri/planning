Alyssa's Goals

Planning for the future

Location

Research and compare different areas of Japan to determine where you would like to live. Consider factors such as cost of living, job opportunities, and quality of life. Start learning about the Japanese job market and research potential employers in your field. Consider networking with other professionals and attending job fairs to learn more about opportunities in Japan.

Moving

Contact a moving company to help you transport your belongings to Japan. Make sure to get multiple quotes and compare prices before choosing a company.

Insurance

Consider purchasing health insurance in Japan to cover any medical expenses you may incur while living in the country.

Confirmation

Confirm your moving arrangements with your chosen moving company.

Flight Prep

Book your flights to Japan and make any necessary travel arrangements. Start packing your belongings and make a list of items you will need to bring with you to Japan.

Bank

Notify your bank, credit card company, and any other relevant financial institutions of your upcoming move. Begin the process of closing any accounts or canceling any services that you will no longer need in the United States.

Confirm

Confirm your travel plans and make any necessary changes.

Paperwork

Complete any necessary paperwork for your move, such as customs forms for your belongings.

Details

Attend to any last-minute details, such as canceling your utilities or updating your mailing address.

Passport

Make sure you have all the necessary documents, such as your passport, ready for your trip.

Goodbyes

Start saying goodbye to friends and family and make plans to stay in touch with them after your move.
